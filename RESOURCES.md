# Chess Resources

## Knowledge

- [Wikipedia: Hippopotamus Defence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hippopotamus_Defence)
  Universal double-fianchetto system (pawns a6/b6/d6/e6/g6/h6, bishops b7/g7, knights d7/e7). Use for: understanding the Hippo structure and why White faces a strategic dilemma after "ideal" development.

- [New in Chess: _The Hippopotamus Defence_ — Alessio de Santis (sample PDF)](https://www.newinchess.com/media/wysiwyg/product_pdf/9081.pdf)
  Club-player manual on Hippo structures, semi-Hippo deviations, and middlegame plan selection. Use for: Hippo-specific lessons after foundational habits are in place.

- [Chess.com: The 1-1-1 Study Plan](https://www.chess.com/article/view/chess-1-1-1-study-plan)
  Minimal viable improvement: 1 puzzle/day, 1 serious game/week, 1 concept/month — guided by your own game review. Use for: keeping study simple when you already play daily.

- [ChessGoals: How to Kill Your Time Trouble](https://chessgoals.com/how-to-kill-your-time-trouble/)
  Time budgeting by game phase, identifying critical moves, leveraging increment. Use for: 10+5 rapid time management (Lesson 2+).

- [JD Chess: How Strong Players Manage the Clock](https://jdchess.com/en/blog/how-strong-players-manage-the-clock)
  Spend time only on moves that commit the next 10–15 moves; play routine moves fast. Use for: separating "think hard" from "just move" moments.

- [Deliberate practice & chess skill (academic study)](https://d-nb.info/1248464257/34)
  Structured, individualized practice with feedback predicts improvement better than unstructured play. Use for: why our lessons focus on one habit at a time with review loops.

- [Chess.com: WGM Belenkaya's Beginner–Intermediate Study Guide (1000–1400)](https://www.chess.com/article/view/wgm-dina-belenkayas-beginner-intermediate-study-guide-1000-1400-elo)
  Rapid games over blitz, daily tactics, strategic principles before opening theory below ~1800. Use for: validating your existing habits and priorities.

## Wisdom (Communities)

- [r/chessbeginners](https://reddit.com/r/chessbeginners)
  Friendly forum for sub-1500 players. Use for: posting games, asking "why did I lose?", Hippo-specific questions.

- [Lichess Study & Practice forums](https://lichess.org/forum)
  Use for: sharing games, finding Hippo study partners, asking about time trouble patterns visible in your Lichess profile.

- [Chess Stack Exchange: What's the best strategy against the Hippo?](https://chess.stackexchange.com/questions/12347/whats-the-best-strategy-against-the-hippo)
  High-reputation Q&A on how opponents try to beat the Hippo. Use for: understanding what you're walking into as a Hippo player.
