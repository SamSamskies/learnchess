# Mission: Get Better at Chess (Hippo Player)

## Why

You enjoy playing chess every day and want your results to match the effort you're already putting in. After a year of daily play and game review, you're still losing points to blunders and time pressure — especially on faster platforms like Duolingo. You also want to get genuinely good at your chosen repertoire: the Hippopotamus, for both colors.

## Success looks like

- Reach **1500 Lichess Rapid** (10+5) without relying on opponents' mistakes
- Stop losing games primarily to **one-move blunders** and **flagging**
- Play the **Hippo with confidence** — knowing typical plans, when to break, and when to stay patient
- Keep chess fun: games, review, Duolingo lessons, and YouTube stay part of the routine

## Constraints

- **1+ hour per day** available for chess
- Prefers learning through **playing games and reviewing them**, plus Duolingo lessons and YouTube
- Plays across time controls: Duolingo (10 min, time-pressured), Lichess blitz/rapid/classical, puzzles
- Current anchors: **~1305 Lichess Rapid (10+5)**, **~1140 Duolingo** (likely depressed by clock)

## Out of scope

- Memorizing deep opening theory lines (Hippo is intentionally low-theory; we focus on structures and plans)
- Grinding bullet as the main training format
- Abandoning the Hippo for a "mainstream" repertoire
