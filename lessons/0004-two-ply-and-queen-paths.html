Two-Ply & Queen Paths

Lesson 4 · ~15 minutes · Skill: think two moves when ahead; spot queen infiltration

Lesson 3 fixed your chain scan. Your last homework showed two new leaks — and one thing you got right that your teacher initially missed.

In o4HNxFX3, you correctly saw that Qxg6+ and Qxd5+ lose your queen to pawn recaptures. That's good calculation. The real miss was playing Qg3 afterward and letting the queen infiltrate via a3 and b2.

In pJ7NicZ9, you saw the hanging pawn on b2 — but grabbed it with your king still on e8.

This lesson adds three filters on top of CCT and the e3-h3-d3 scan. They take a few extra seconds only when they apply.

1. Two-ply on check-captures

A capture with + is not automatically good. Before you click, whisper:

I take → they take → then what? Critical speed · ~5 seconds · only when you're considering a capture with check

Quiet pawn captures often beat flashy queen checks when the take-back is ugly. You already proved you can do this math — the habit is running it every time you eye a check-capture.

2. Queen path scan (Threats step)

Lesson 3 covers Captures on your chain. This covers Threats when you've pushed flank pawns (a3, b3) or broken with d4:

b2 · d4 · a3 · h4 Threats step · 1 second each · "where can their queen land in one move?"
  1. a3 — flank pawn you pushed; queen on d6 or c5 often eyes it along the diagonal.
  2. b2 — infiltration square. After ...Qxa3, ...Qb2 is the classic follow-up.
  3. d4 — center pawn after your d4 break; queen lands here with tempo.
  4. h4 — kingside entry (your win in v3TjizDT started when their queen reached h4).

Run this during the Threats step of CCT — especially when you're ahead and tempted to play a quiet retreat.

3. King-safe gate before grabs

King safe? → then grab. Before any capture when your king hasn't castled into a corner

At move 24 in pJ7NicZ9, ...Bxb2 won a pawn and opened c3 — but your king was on e8 with White's queen on f3. One question would have flagged it:

"Where does my king go if they check me?" If you can't answer, don't grab.
