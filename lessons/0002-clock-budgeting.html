Clock Budgeting

Lesson 2 · ~15 minutes · Skill: three speeds + the move-15 checkpoint

Your homework showed something important: you were doing CCT — but it turned into a long think. Time anxiety kicked in. You rushed the scan, missed Captures on e3 and h3, and blundered anyway. Game 2 was the flags-and-blunders double loss your mission is trying to kill.

This lesson teaches one skill: how fast to move in a 10+5 rapid game, so CCT stays a 3-second safety scan and you still have minutes when the Hippo fight gets real.

10+5 in plain numbers

You start with 10 minutes (600 seconds). Every move you make adds 5 seconds. A typical game runs 50–70 moves, so your "real" budget is roughly 12–16 minutes if you use the increment — but only if you don't burn the bank early.

Strong players don't spend evenly. They play fast when little is at stake and slow when one move locks in the next ten. (JD Chess on clock management)

Three speeds

Before each move, ask: what kind of moment is this? Pick a speed first. Then think. Then CCT. Then click.

≤10 sec Book speed A move you've played a hundred times in your Hippo — fianchetto, d3, Nd2, h3, O-O. No calculation needed. Recognize, play, scan, go.
15–30 sec Normal speed Quiet development. Obvious recaptures. Opponent mirrors your setup. Pick a reasonable plan, don't hunt for the engine's #1.
45–120 sec Critical speed Pawn breaks, piece trades, king safety, or tactics. This is where you spend the time you saved in the opening. One good think here is worth five minutes of opening hesitation. (ChessGoals on critical moves)

Print the speeds: Clock Budget reference card.

The move-15 checkpoint

After your opening, glance at the clock.

≥ 6:00 remaining

If you're below 6 minutes at move ~15, you spent critical-speed time on book-speed moves.

Your White Hippo development — g3 Bg2 d3 Nd2 e3 h3 a3 b4 Bb2 O-O — should mostly be book speed. That's how you bank time for the moment someone plays ...f5 or offers a trade on e3.

Two players reach move 15 (same position, same 10+5)
Player A — book speed in opening7:40 left
Player B — 45 sec per opening move3:20 left

Player B is you in game 2 — enough time to panic, not enough to calculate. Player A has room for one proper critical think and a 3-second CCT on every move.

CCT is not a speed

This is the fix for your homework problem. CCT from Lesson 1 is not thinking time. It's a fixed 3-second add-on at every speed:

  1. Pick your speed (book / normal / critical)
  2. Think only that long to choose a move
  3. Whisper "checks, captures, threats" — 3 seconds
  4. Click

If CCT is taking 30+ seconds, you're calculating disguised as scanning. Stop. Either upgrade the moment to critical speed and think properly, or pick the obvious move and scan quickly.

Practice: pick the speed

Your homework (next 3 rapid games)

  2. Tag one critical moment per game — the move where you spent 45+ seconds. Was it worth it?
