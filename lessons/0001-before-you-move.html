Before You Move

Lesson 1 · ~15 minutes · Skill: the CCT safety check

You play every day. You review your games. You still blunder. That usually means one thing: the mistake happens before you move, not because you lack chess knowledge.

This lesson teaches one habit — used by strong players under time pressure — that takes about 5–10 seconds and catches most one-move blunders. It connects directly to your mission: fewer thrown games, better Duolingo results, and more patience in cramped Hippo positions where one slip collapses everything.

The habit: CCT

C · C · T

Checks · Captures · Threats

After you choose a move — but before you click it — ask what your opponent can do on their next turn:

  1. Checks — Can they give check? If yes, is my king safe after?
  2. Captures — Can they take a piece or pawn I left hanging?
  3. Threats — Can they fork, pin, skewer, or threaten mate in one?

This is not deep calculation. It's a safety scan. Research on chess improvement consistently shows that structured, feedback-driven practice beats passive study — and in your games, the fastest feedback is not losing the piece you just hung. (deliberate practice study)

Keep a printed copy handy: Move Check reference card.

Practice: spot the blunder waiting to happen

Question 1 of 3

Your homework (next 3 games)

  1. Before every move on Duolingo or Lichess, whisper "checks, captures, threats" once.
  2. After each game, find one blunder and write: "I moved ___. I didn't check for ___."
  3. Bring that note back to your teacher — we'll see if the pattern is tactics, time, or Hippo-specific.

Why this comes before Hippo theory

The Hippo rewards patience — you wait behind your pawn wall while White figures out how to attack. But cramped positions are unforgiving: one loose piece and the position "collapses" (common anti-Hippo strategy). CCT is the seatbelt for that style.

Lesson 2 will cover clock budgeting for 10+5 — how to save time in your Hippo setup so you still have minutes when the real fight starts.
