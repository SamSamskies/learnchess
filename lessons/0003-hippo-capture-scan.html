Hippo Capture Scan

Lesson 3 · ~15 minutes · Skill: the e3-h3-d3 captures drill

Lesson 1 gave you CCT. Lesson 2 gave you clock budgeting. Your homework in both showed the same blind spot: you run the habit, but the Captures step skips the pawns your Hippo leaves on e3 and h3. Those aren't deep tactics — they're diagonal snipers you looked past.

This lesson replaces vague "check captures" with one concrete drill: point at three squares, one second each. It plugs directly into CCT and takes three seconds — not thirty.

Your pawn chain is the target

White Hippo setup: g3 Bg2 d3 e3 h3. Opponents learn to aim at the chain, not your pieces. Your Lesson 1 blunders were both chain captures:

The drill: e3 · h3 · d3

During the Captures step of CCT, don't scan the whole board. Run this instead:

e3 → h3 → d3 1 second per square · trace the diagonal or file outward
  1. e3 — follow the long diagonal toward a7/c5. Pawn breaks like f4 can open it.
  2. h3 — check the c8 bishop (often still undeveloped!) and the g7 fianchetto.
  3. d3 — queen to d3/c4, bishops on b5/a6. Re-scan after g4.

Pair it with clock speed

From Lesson 2: pawn breaks and piece moves that open lines are critical speed moments. Think at critical speed, then run e3-h3-d3, then click. The scan is not thinking time — it's three seconds on top.

If you play f4 or Nd2 at book speed and skip the scan, you convert a saved minute into a lost pawn and a lost king.

Your homework (next 3 rapid games)

  1. During every Captures step, whisper "e3, h3, d3" and point at each square on the board.
  2. After pawn breaks (f4, g4, b4) or knight moves (Nd2, Ne2), run the scan twice — diagonals shift when pieces move.
  3. If you miss or catch a chain capture, write: "Move ___. Square ___. Piece ___."
  4. Bring your three games back to your teacher.
