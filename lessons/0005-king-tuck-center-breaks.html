King Tuck & Center Breaks

Lesson 5 · ~12 minutes · Skill: tuck the king before you punch in the center

Lesson 4 homework: two wins, one demolition. Games 1 and 2 show your new filters working — queen path at move 16, two-ply at move 56. Game 3 was an opening collapse after 12. Bxe6, before any middlegame habit could help.

This lesson covers what game 1 got right and game 3 needed earlier: where the king goes before you break in the center.

1. King tuck (Black Modern / Hippo)

When you've castled kingside and White's queen sits on d2 or f3 with a bishop on c4/a2, your king on g8 sits on the same diagonal as e6. Before you play ...e5 or open the center, tuck:

Kh8 · Kh7 Before ...e5 or ...c5 when Q+B aim at your kingside · ~2 seconds

In UC1l19yg you played 12...Kh7 — exactly this. White's queen was on d2, bishop on b3 heading to a2. Only after the tuck did you prepare ...e5 and lift the rook with ...Rac8.

2. The wall test — when is the break safe?

g6 · h6 · f7 · e7 — all still there? If yes, center break is on the menu. If a pawn moved, re-check king tuck first.

Your Hippo pawn wall (g6/h6/d6/e6 as Black) is what makes patience work. You can wait, tuck the king, then strike with ...e5 or ...c5 when White overextends. If you've already pushed ...f5 or traded on e6, the wall is cracked — don't break until you've recalculated.

3. Qf3 gate (from game 3)

Against 3. Qf3, 3...e6 alone leaves f7 thin and invites Bxe6 later. The gate:

3. Qf3...Nf6Blocks f7; develops with tempo

In lcdaCOns, 12. Bxe6 was a Captures hit on your own pawn — the same chain logic as Lesson 3, but the target is e6 instead of e3.

4. White Hippo tuck (brief)

As White in the Hungarian, the mirror moves are Kh1 and Rf1 — tuck the king off the g1 diagonal before you play f4 or open the e-file. You'll see this in future homework when you're White.
